JUSTIN MINH / / year 3 / Rewarded Patience

drawings

The experience of a temporary twilight that envelopes the Earth is an rare occurance that many wait in anticipation for. We look up in the sky with apparatus to see the beauty of the ring of fire that sits heavenly in the sky that blesses us for a moment before it disappears into the everyday of our lives. This phenomem brings people in clusters, in preparation and anticipation to observe such magnificance despite the slow moving nature of the moon and sun's orbit. The patience of the masses can lead into a moment of bliss or it instills this growing discomfort that creeps up on you evolving into a physical sensation that pushes you to give up on the wait. Time is lost while we wait, with the unknown expectation of what is to be obtained.

The ideas of what could of been experienced create conflict over the cost time. Yet imagine, if the eclipse aligns itself at the same time of a timed event like your own graduation and everyone looks up at the sky instead of your certificate acceptance, what is lost in the experience of one may not be obtained again.Those that sit true to patience are rewarded, however those that give up can go about and seek the mundane joy that the everyday offers them.

The active reality sits behind you, giving you glimpses of what is happening in real-time. Using the telescope to see the complete eclipse requires patience however to decide if it worth your time is up to you.
