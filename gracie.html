GRACIE MAY / year 3 / Interconnected Cycles

drawings

'Interconnected Cycles' highlights the dual representation of micro and macro time cycles juxtaposed on interactive acrylic layers at different scales.

On one side, the silhouettes of a person's growth from childhood to adulthood, and on the other, a depiction of the cell cycle, illuminated by a pulsing light to link both cycles back to the objective pulse of the human body which fuels them despite their differences.

This installation encourages the user to reflect on the intricate connections between the micro and macro aspects of existence, conceptually aligning with Carlo Rovelli's idea of 'The Granularity of Time' by presenting time as a series of interconnected but potentially discrete layers.
